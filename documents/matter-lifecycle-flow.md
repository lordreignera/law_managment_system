# Matter Lifecycle Flow

This document records the proposed Kalikumutima matter lifecycle and how it should be implemented in the application.

## Proposed Status Flow

Inquiry -> Consultation -> Conflict Check -> Engagement Pending -> Open -> Planning -> Active -> Waiting for Client -> Waiting for Third Party/Court -> Billing Pending -> Under Review -> Closed -> Archived

## Business Stages

1. Client Intake
   - Capture enquiry, client profile, contact details, conflict parties, legal issue, preferred lawyer, urgency, referral source, uploaded documents, KYC, and consultation appointment.
   - Outcomes: reject, schedule consultation, or proceed to conflict check.

2. Consultation
   - Lawyer meets the client, gathers facts, reviews documents, gives preliminary advice, and estimates scope and fees.

3. Conflict Check
   - Search existing clients, matters, related parties, and opponents.
   - Record cleared or failed decision with reasons.

4. Engagement
   - Generate engagement letter, fee agreement, retainer request, client acceptance, and open the client account.

5. Matter Opening
   - Generate matter number, assign category, practice area, partner, associates, privacy level, opposing counsel, shelf, and status.

6. Matter Planning
   - Create milestones, tasks, hearings, deadlines, team assignments, document checklist, risk assessment, and budget.

7. Active Matter Work
   - Track notes, documents, correspondence, emails, phone logs, meetings, research, drafting, evidence, witness management, filings, approvals, and client updates.

8. Hearings / Transactions
   - Manage court appearances, negotiations, ADR, settlements, conveyancing, registrations, and company filings depending on matter type.

9. Financial Management
   - Track time, expenses, invoices, trust funds where applicable, receipts, payments, and outstanding balances.

10. Collaboration
   - Support internal notes, approvals, delegated work, notifications, reminders, and audit trail.

11. Client Portal
   - Share matter progress, documents, invoices, required actions, secure messaging, and appointments.

12. Review
   - Partner quality review, deliverables confirmation, billing confirmation, and client feedback.

13. Matter Closure
   - Record outcome, close tasks, issue final invoice, and create closing letter.

14. Archiving
   - Archive digital and physical files, retention schedule, restore capability, and archived search.

## Current Implementation Decision

The first implementation step is to make the lifecycle statuses first-class on matters. New matters can now be created with the proposed lifecycle status, and the matter register can filter and display those statuses.

Client intake and conflict review are now implemented as the first workflow gate before a matter is opened. Intake records capture enquiry details, consultation timing, preferred lawyer, practice area, urgency, referral source, and conflict parties. A reviewer can mark the conflict review as pending, cleared, conflict found, or more information needed. Only cleared intakes can be converted into engagement-pending matters.

The normal user-facing workflow starts from client intake. Client and matter registers no longer present the main action as manual client or matter creation; they guide users back to intake. Manual creation routes may remain available for administration, imports, or cleanup, but the standard workflow is intake-first.

Engagement review is now implemented through a separate engagements table linked to the approved client and matter. Before a matter becomes open, the firm records the engagement type, engagement letter date, fee agreement date, retainer details, client acceptance date, and engagement notes. Accepting engagement moves the matter from `Engagement Pending` to `Open`.

Approved clients now act as the ongoing client-management hub. From the approved client register, users can view client details, add more details, or add a new engagement for repeat work. A new engagement for an existing client creates an engagement record and an engagement-pending matter, then follows the same engagement review process before the matter is opened.

Register numbers are generated by the system and are not editable by users. The format is `PREFIX + YY + MM + 4-digit monthly sequence`, for example `CI26060001`. Prefixes are `CI` for client intakes, `CL` for clients, and `MT` for matters. The sequence starts again from `0001` each new month.

System setting and setup codes are also generated by the system and are not editable by users. These use the company initials plus the table prefix and sequence, for example `KC-IT-0001` for an instruction type. This applies to system setting records and setup tables such as branches, departments, recovery clients, and zonal offices.

## Recommended Next Build Order

1. Matter status transitions
   - Add controlled transition buttons instead of free status selection.
   - Record status history with changed by, changed at, from status, to status, and notes.

2. Planning and work execution
   - Add milestones, tasks, deadlines, document checklist, hearings, and reminders.

3. Closure and archive controls
   - Require billing check, task completion, outcome, closing letter, and archive metadata before final archive.

4. Permissions and audit
   - Add stage-specific permissions and audit logs for sensitive workflow changes.
